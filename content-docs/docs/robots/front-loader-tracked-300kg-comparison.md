## Bobcat Company History and Acquisition

### Bobcat Becomes Doosan Bobcat (2007)

**Doosan Group acquired Bobcat Company in 2007** from Ingersoll Rand, which at the time was the largest overseas acquisition by a Korean company. The acquisition included:

- Bobcat Company and its manufacturing facilities
- Complete product lines (skid-steer loaders, compact track loaders, mini excavators, etc.)
- Bobcat brand and dealer network
- Engineering and manufacturing expertise

### Post-Acquisition Developments

Following the acquisition, Bobcat operated as **Doosan Bobcat** under the Doosan Group umbrella. However, the company continued to use the "Bobcat" brand name for its products and marketing, with "Doosan Bobcat" used primarily in corporate contexts.

**Key Milestones:**
- **2007**: Doosan acquires Bobcat from Ingersoll Rand
- **2021**: Doosan Group sold Doosan Infracore (construction division) to Hyundai (now known as Develon) due to financial pressures from the Bobcat acquisition debt
- **2024**: Announcement of merger between Bobcat and Doosan Robotics

### Bobcat Market Position

- **Global Ranking**: 10th largest equipment manufacturer in the world (2023 KHL Yellow Table, up from 11th in 2022)
- **Market Share**: Significant share in compact track loader and skid-steer loader markets in North America
- **Product Lines**: Skid-steer loaders, compact track loaders, mini excavators, compact tractors, attachments
- **Dealer Network**: Extensive North American and global dealer network

**Note:** Specific market share percentages for compact track loaders are proprietary industry data, but Bobcat is recognized as one of the top 3 manufacturers in the compact loader segment alongside Caterpillar and Kubota.

## Bobcat/Doosan Robotics Merger and Strategic Direction

### Corporate Restructuring (2024)

In July 2024, Bobcat's parent company Doosan Group announced plans to merge Bobcat with Doosan Robotics, creating a new entity focused on autonomous and automated construction equipment. This strategic move positions the combined company to:

- **Advance Autonomous Machines**: Accelerate development of autonomous construction equipment
- **Expand Robot Market Presence**: Enter the high-growth robot industry market
- **Capture Automation Megatrend**: Leverage the "autonomous/automation megatrend" in construction
- **Integrate Technologies**: Combine Bobcat's equipment manufacturing expertise with Doosan Robotics' automation capabilities

**Source:** [Total Landscape Care - Bobcat Plans Merger with Doosan Robotics](https://www.totallandscapecare.com/business/article/15680346/bobcat-plans-merger-with-doosan-robotics-to-advance-autonomous-machines)

### Merger Details

- **Timeline**: Announced July 2024, expected completion by end of 2024 (subject to shareholder approval)
- **Structure**: Doosan Robotics will obtain Bobcat's shares in a stock swap
- **New Entity**: Doosan Robotics would become the merged company, representing about 42% of Doosan Group
- **Strategic Pillar**: Falls under Doosan Group's "Smart Machine" pillar, focusing on autonomous construction and industrial vehicles
- **Challenges**: Some shareholders and lawmakers expressed concerns about the 1-for-0.63 stock swap ratio

#### T7X - World's First All-Electric Compact Track Loader

Bobcat introduced the **T7X** as the world's first all-electric compact track loader, representing a complete redesign of compact loader technology.

**Official Specifications** ([Bobcat T7X Product Page](https://www.bobcat.com/na/en/equipment/loaders/compact-track-loaders/t7x)):

**Power & Performance:**
- **Horsepower**: 100 hp
- **Battery**: 72.6-kilowatt-hour lithium-ion battery
- **System Voltage**: 465V
- **Runtime**: Up to 6 hours of continuous runtime
- **Charging Time**: 12 hours with 240V, 40A outlet (J1772 EVSE)
- **Charging Rate**: 6 kilowatts
- **Motor Type**: Permanent Magnet (Brushless)

**Performance Specifications:**
- **Rated Operating Capacity (ISO)**: 3,056 lbs (1,387 kg)
- **Operating Weight**: 12,590 lbs (5,710 kg)
- **Breakout Force - Tilt**: 8,218 lbs (machine limitation)
- **Breakout Force - Lift**: 8,118 lbs (machine limitation)
- **Tipping Load**: 8,743 lbs
- **Travel Speed**: 5.7 mph (low) / 8.7 mph (high)
- **Ground Pressure (Rubber)**: 5.2 psi

**Noise Levels:**
- **Operator Position Noise**: 65.4 dB (per ISO 6396/ISO 4871)
- **Bystander Noise Level**: 92.7 dB (per ISO 6395)

**Key Technologies:**
- **No Hydraulics**: Completely eliminates traditional hydraulic systems
- **Ball-Screw Actuators**: Four ball-screw actuators replace hydraulic cylinders
- **Instantaneous Torque**: Up to 3 times greater than traditional loaders
- **Power Management System**: Intelligent system preserves runtime and battery life
- **Three Control Modes**: Eco, Normal, and Peak power modes
- **Three Control Response Modes**: Adjustable work group and drive response

**Charging System:**
- **Charging Interface**: J1772 EVSE (Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment standard)
- **Voltage**: Up to 240V, 40A grid service
- **Charging Time**: Approximately 12 hours for full charge
- **Charging Rate**: 6 kilowatts

**Additional Features:**
- **Machine IQ**: Wireless communications for remote monitoring and fleet management
- **8-Inch Display**: Full-featured machine interaction and monitoring
- **Premium LED Lights**: Front, rear, and side lights with blue strobe lighting
- **Immediate Cold Weather Performance**: No engine warmup required
- **Fewer Components**: Nearly 50% reduction in components vs. diesel loaders

**Advantages:**
- Zero emissions operation
- Near-silent operation with low vibration
- Reduced maintenance costs (no hydraulic fluid, fewer components)
- Instantaneous torque and precise control
- Software updates via wireless connectivity
- Lower operating costs (full charge costs ~$12 in U.S.)
